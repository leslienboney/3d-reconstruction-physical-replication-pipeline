# Reconstruction Analysis

## Overview

This project compares COLMAP reconstruction performance under two very different conditions:

1. High-quality dataset (South Building)
2. Low-quality dataset (car captured at night)

The goal was to understand not just how the pipeline works, but when and why it fails.

---

## South Building (Controlled Dataset)

- Input images: 127
- Registered images: 127
- Points:
  - Exhaustive: 216,792
  - Sequential: 85,203
- Runtime: ~2.5 minutes

### Observations

- Both methods reconstruct the full structure
- Exhaustive produces higher density
- Sequential slightly reduces coverage but remains stable

This shows what COLMAP can do when feature quality is high and consistent.

---

## Car Dataset (Real-World Failure Case)

- Input images: 155
- Registered images:
  - Exhaustive: 83
  - Sequential: 52
  - Guided: 51
- Points:
  - Best case: 6,607
  - Worst case: ~1,750

### Observations

- Reconstruction collapses early
- Large portions of geometry are missing
- Point cloud is extremely sparse

---

## Why It Failed

The failure is almost entirely due to input conditions:

- Low light → fewer detectable features
- Motion blur → unstable keypoints
- Reflective surfaces → inconsistent feature matches
- Repeated angles → weak geometric constraints

These break the multi-view matching pipeline.

---

## Key Insight

Even though each image contained ~1500–3000 keypoints, very few could be matched across multiple views.

Result:
- High 2D keypoints
- Very low 3D reconstruction

---

## Takeaway

COLMAP does not fail randomly.

It fails when:
- feature quality is low
- overlap between images is weak
- geometry cannot be consistently triangulated

This project made it clear that reconstruction quality is driven more by **data capture** than algorithm choice.
